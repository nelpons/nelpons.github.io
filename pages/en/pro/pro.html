My Journey

Education, internships, experience, PhD, publications and awards

My Objectives

From the beginning of my academic journey, I have oriented my choices towards high-performance materials and technically demanding environments. My scientific curiosity and constant desire to learn have naturally led me towards ambitious projects, often in international contexts. Each experience — in laboratories, industry, or research centres — has strengthened my determination to understand, design and optimise materials capable of meeting extreme constraints.

This guiding thread has taken me across different countries, collaborating with multicultural teams and exploring a wide range of fields: additive manufacturing, functional polymers, materials mechanics, and space technologies. Today, I continue to move forward with the same motivation: to learn, to share knowledge, and to contribute to projects that push the boundaries of what is possible.

Academic and Professional Choices

My academic journey has been shaped by diverse experiences, deliberate choices, and transitions that have influenced the way I learn and work. Originally from Mandelieu-la-Napoule, I began my secondary education in Cannes before completing my final years at the Military High School of Aix-en-Provence, where I obtained a scientific baccalaureate (major in Life and Earth Sciences) in 2013.

Driven by a strong interest in life sciences, I then joined Lyon 1 University to complete two years of medical studies and sit the highly competitive PACES examination. This demanding period provided me with strong work discipline, excellent learning and concentration abilities, and a solid understanding of human physiology. It also made me realise that I deeply missed the rigour of mathematics and the logic of physics, and that I wanted to pursue a path as an engineer and researcher.

I then joined Polytech Lyon in the second year of the integrated preparatory cycle (PEIP2 – post-PACES) in 2015–2016, before specialising in Physical Engineering and continuing my engineering studies at Polytech Clermont-Ferrand from 2016 onwards. These years were decisive: they confirmed my interest in science, allowed me to explore various technical fields, and helped me develop strong scientific versatility.

Beyond coursework, I took every opportunity through internships and projects to travel, discover new cultures, improve my English, and work in diverse environments. These experiences led me to a key decision: pursuing a double degree in Canada, extending my studies by six months but providing a unique international experience and a Master’s degree in addition to my engineering qualification.
